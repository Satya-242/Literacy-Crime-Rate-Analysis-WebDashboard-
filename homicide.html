What is Homicide?

Homicide is the act of one person killing another. It includes:

  • Lawful killings (e.g., self-defense, legal law enforcement actions)
  • Unlawful killings (e.g., murder, manslaughter)

In crime statistics, we usually focus on intentional homicides, which are unlawful and deliberate.

How is Homicide Rate Calculated?

The homicide rate helps compare crime across places by adjusting for population size. It's calculated as: 

Homicide Rate = (Number of intentional homicides / Total population) × 100,000

Example:

If a country has 500 intentional homicides and a population of 10,000,000:

Homicide Rate = (500 / 10,000,000) × 100,000 = 5 per 100,000 people

Why Per 100,000?

It standardizes the rate, allowing fair comparisons across countries or regions of different sizes.

Common Data Sources:

  • UNODC (United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime)
  • WHO (World Health Organization)
  • National crime bureaus or police departments
