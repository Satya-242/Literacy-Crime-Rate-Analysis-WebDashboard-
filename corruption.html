What is Corruption?

Corruption refers to the abuse of entrusted power for private gain. It can take many forms and often occurs in both public and private sectors.

  • Bribery – offering or receiving something of value to influence actions
  • Embezzlement – stealing or misappropriating funds placed in one’s trust
  • Favoritism & Nepotism – unfair advantages given to friends or relatives
  • Extortion – obtaining something through force or threats

How is Corruption Measured?

Unlike crimes like theft or homicide, corruption is often hidden and difficult to detect directly. So, it's typically measured using **perception-based indices**:

  • Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI) – Published by Transparency International
  • World Bank’s Control of Corruption Indicator
  • Global Corruption Barometer

Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI)

The CPI ranks countries on a scale from 0 to 100:

  • 0 = Highly corrupt
  • 100 = Very clean

It is based on expert assessments and opinion surveys.

Why is it Hard to Measure?

Corruption often goes unreported due to fear, political influence, or lack of evidence. Therefore, perception and indirect metrics are widely used.
