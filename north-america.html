About North America

North America is the third-largest continent in the world, located entirely in the Northern Hemisphere and almost all within the Western Hemisphere. It is bordered by the Arctic Ocean to the north, the Atlantic Ocean to the east, the Pacific Ocean to the west, and South America to the south.

Key Facts:

  • Area: Approximately 24.71 million square kilometers
  • Population: Over 590 million people
  • Number of countries: 23 sovereign countries and many territories
  • Largest country: Canada (by area), United States (by population)
  • Languages: English, Spanish, French, and many Indigenous languages

Major Countries in North America:

  • United States
  • Canada
  • Mexico
  • Guatemala
  • Cuba
  • Honduras
  • Panama
  • Jamaica
  • Haiti
  • Dominican Republic

Interesting Points:

  • Home to some of the world’s largest economies (USA and Canada)
  • Features a diverse climate and geography — from Arctic tundra to tropical beaches
  • Rich cultural diversity shaped by Indigenous, European, African, and Asian influences
  • Hosts some of the world's most visited natural landmarks, like Niagara Falls and the Grand Canyon

Challenges:

  • Immigration and border issues
  • Climate change impacts, including hurricanes and wildfires
  • Crime and urban violence in certain areas
  • Economic disparities and social inequality
