What is Sexual Violence?

Sexual violence refers to any sexual act or attempt to obtain a sexual act through coercion, force, threats, or manipulation. It includes:

  • Rape – non-consensual sexual intercourse
  • Sexual assault – unwanted sexual contact or behavior
  • Harassment – unwelcome sexual advances, comments, or requests
  • Exploitation – abusing power for sexual gain

How is Sexual Violence Measured?

Sexual violence is measured based on reported cases, but many incidents go unreported due to stigma, fear, or lack of support. Common metrics include:

  • Number of reported rape or assault cases annually
  • Prevalence rates from surveys and health studies
  • Police or legal system records

Formula to Calculate Reported Rate:

Sexual Violence Rate = (Number of reported sexual violence cases / Total population) × 100,000

Example:

If a region has 300 reported cases and a population of 2,000,000:

Sexual Violence Rate = (300 / 2,000,000) × 100,000 = 15 per 100,000 people

Why It’s Underreported

Many survivors do not report due to:

  • Fear of retaliation
  • Shame or social stigma
  • Lack of trust in law enforcement or justice system

Common Data Sources:

  • National Crime Records Bureau (NCRB)
  • World Health Organization (WHO)
  • UN Women and UNICEF Reports
  • Local police departments and NGOs
