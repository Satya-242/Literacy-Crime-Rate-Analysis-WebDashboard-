What is Theft?

Theft is the act of unlawfully taking someone else's property with the intent to permanently deprive them of it. It can include various types such as:

  • Petty theft – stealing items of low value
  • Grand theft – stealing high-value items
  • Burglary – unlawful entry into a building to commit theft
  • Robbery – theft involving force or threat of force

How is Theft Rate Calculated?

To compare theft incidents across regions, the theft rate is calculated per 100,000 people: 

Theft Rate = (Number of reported thefts / Total population) × 100,000

Example:

If a city has 2,000 reported thefts in a year and a population of 500,000:

Theft Rate = (2,000 / 500,000) × 100,000 = 400 per 100,000 people

Why Per 100,000?

This standard unit helps compare the frequency of theft across cities or countries regardless of population size.

Common Data Sources:

  • National police reports and crime bureaus
  • UNODC (United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime)
  • Local or regional crime monitoring authorities
